# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

Meridian Mutual provides PTO for vacation, personal time, and short illness. PTO is separate from the company holiday calendar and from sick leave where state law requires it (e.g., NY ESSTA, WI municipal ordinances).

## Accrual Schedule

PTO accrues per pay period and is based on continuous service:

| Years of service | Annual PTO |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | 15 days |
| 4–9 | 20 days |
| 10+ | 25 days |

Accrual stops at **1.5x** the annual entitlement. Employees should plan time off so the cap is not reached; the cap does not retroactively reset.

## Carryover

Up to **5 days** of unused PTO carry into the next calendar year. Carryover days must be used by **March 31** or are forfeited. The cap does not apply where state law requires otherwise.

## Holidays

Meridian Mutual observes 10 paid holidays plus one **floating holiday** and a **paid birthday-off** day. The current calendar is posted on HR Hub each November.

## Requesting Time Off

1. Submit requests via Workday at least **7 calendar days** in advance for stays of 3+ days.
2. Same-day requests for personal illness should be entered by end of business that day.
3. Manager approval is required; conflicts are resolved by seniority and prior approval order.

## Blackout Periods

The Investments and Finance teams observe a blackout window during fiscal year close (the last 10 business days of December). Other departments do not have blackouts.

## Separation

Wisconsin, New York, and California employees receive payout of accrued unused PTO at separation. Other states follow company practice of forfeiture unless required by law. Final payout is processed in the next regular payroll cycle following the separation date.

For questions, contact HR Benefits or extension 4400.
