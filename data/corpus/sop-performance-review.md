# Performance Review — Standard Operating Procedure

Meridian Mutual operates on an annual performance cycle with a structured mid-year touchpoint.

## Cycle Calendar

- **February 1 – March 15**: Annual review window for prior calendar year
- **April**: Compensation cycle (merit, promotion, equity refresh)
- **July**: Mid-year check-in
- **October**: Goal-setting refresh for the next year

## Ratings

A 5-point scale calibrated by Director-level and above:

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| 5 | Significantly exceeds expectations across all dimensions |
| 4 | Exceeds expectations |
| 3 | Meets expectations |
| 2 | Inconsistently meets expectations — calibration required |
| 1 | Does not meet expectations |

A rating of **2 or 1** automatically triggers a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** initiated by the manager and HR Business Partner within 14 days.

## Process

1. **Self-review due** five business days before the manager review (employee writes to the Workday template).
2. **Manager review** drafted in Workday; calibration with peer managers happens before release.
3. **Calibration** by Director and above — adjustments documented.
4. **Delivery meeting** scheduled by manager. The conversation, rating, and forward goals must be discussed.
5. **Acknowledgement** by employee in Workday within 14 days.

## Competency Framework

Reviews assess against five competencies, weighted equally:

- Client Focus
- Operational Rigor
- Coaching and Development
- Innovation
- Risk Awareness

Each competency has role-specific behavioral anchors maintained by People Analytics in HR Hub > Performance > Competencies.

## Goals

Goals are tracked in Workday Goals. Employees should have **3–5 goals per cycle**, with at least one tied to a team OKR. Goals can be edited mid-cycle with manager approval.

## Compensation Linkage

Merit increases, target bonuses, and equity refreshes use the prior cycle's rating as the primary input. Final amounts are determined at the April Compensation Committee meeting.
