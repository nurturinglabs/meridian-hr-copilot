# 401(k) Guide — New York Participants

This guide supplements **faq-401k** with state-specific notes for employees who reside or are taxed in New York.

## New York State Tax Treatment

New York generally conforms to federal treatment of 401(k) contributions. Pre-tax contributions reduce New York taxable wages. Roth 401(k) contributions are taxed when made; qualified Roth distributions are not taxed at distribution.

## Pension and Annuity Income Exclusion

For taxpayers age **59½ and older**, New York allows an exclusion of up to **$20,000 per year** of qualifying pension and annuity income — **including 401(k) distributions** — from New York State income tax. Distributions taken before 59½ **do not qualify** for this exclusion.

## NYC Resident Tax

Employees who reside in any of the five boroughs are subject to NYC resident income tax on 401(k) distributions, with the same federal-conformity treatment. There is no NYC pension exclusion equivalent to the state exclusion.

## NYSLRS Rollovers

Employees with service in the **New York State and Local Retirement System** (NYSLRS) — including Tier 6 — may roll over eligible balances into the Meridian Mutual 401(k) plan. The required form is **RS-5420**, available from osc.state.ny.us. Coordinate with Total Rewards before submission.

## SALT and High-Earner Considerations

For New York participants subject to the federal SALT cap, maximizing pre-tax 401(k) contributions and considering the **after-tax / mega-backdoor Roth** path may be more tax-efficient than equivalent taxable savings. Consult a qualified tax advisor.
