# Meridian Mutual 401(k) Plan — FAQ

## When am I eligible?

You are eligible to contribute on your **first day of employment**. Auto-enrollment begins on day 31 unless you opt out earlier in Fidelity NetBenefits.

## What are the default settings?

The default contribution rate is **6% pre-tax**, with a **1% annual auto-escalation** to a ceiling of 12%. You can change your rate, election (pre-tax or Roth), and investments at any time at netbenefits.com.

## What is the company match?

Meridian Mutual matches **dollar-for-dollar up to 5%** of eligible compensation. Match contributions vest after **2 years of continuous service** (cliff). Your own contributions are always 100% vested.

## What investment options are available?

The lineup includes Fidelity Freedom Index target-date funds (default), a US/international/bond index sleeve, an actively managed core bond fund, and a self-directed brokerage window with a $250 annual fee.

## Can I take a loan or hardship withdrawal?

Yes — both are allowed within IRS limits (loans up to 50% of vested balance or $50,000, whichever is less; hardships per safe-harbor reasons). Apply through NetBenefits; loans are typically funded within 5 business days.

## Roth and after-tax options?

Roth 401(k) is available. After-tax contributions and in-plan Roth conversions ("mega backdoor") are also permitted up to the IRS section 415 limit.

## What happens if I leave Meridian Mutual?

You may keep the balance in the plan, roll it over to an IRA or new employer plan, or take a distribution (subject to taxes and possible penalty). Vested balances under $7,000 may be force-rolled to a Fidelity IRA after 90 days.

## State-specific guidance

Refer to **guide-401k-wi** or **guide-401k-ny** for state tax notes and local Fidelity contacts.
