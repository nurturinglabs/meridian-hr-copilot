# Bereavement Leave Policy

Meridian Mutual provides paid bereavement leave so employees can attend services, manage estate matters, and grieve without using PTO.

## Allotment

- **Immediate family** (spouse or domestic partner, child, parent, sibling, parent-in-law, child-in-law, sibling-in-law): up to **5 paid days** per loss
- **Extended family** (grandparent, grandchild, aunt, uncle, niece, nephew): up to **3 paid days** per loss
- **Close friend or colleague**: up to **1 unpaid day** with manager approval
- **Pregnancy loss** of self or partner: up to **5 paid days**, in addition to any medical leave

## How to Use

1. Notify your manager as soon as practical.
2. Log the absence in Workday under Time Off → Bereavement; no documentation is required at intake, though HR may request later for audit purposes.
3. Days may be taken non-consecutively within 60 days of the loss to accommodate travel, services, and estate matters.

## Travel Support

For deaths requiring international travel, employees may request up to **3 additional unpaid days** plus access to the company's emergency travel assistance program (International SOS). Submit Form MM-BR-200.

## Extension Beyond Five Days

Employees needing additional time should speak with their manager about FMLA-eligible leave (where qualifying conditions exist) or unpaid personal leave. The Employee Assistance Program (EAP) through ComPsych offers grief counseling at no cost — call (800) 555-0150 or use code MERIDIAN at compsych.com.

## Tax Considerations

Bereavement pay is taxable as ordinary wages. State-specific rules may apply for survivor benefits paid through life insurance; consult a tax advisor.

For questions, contact HR Benefits at benefits@meridianmutual.com or extension 4400.
