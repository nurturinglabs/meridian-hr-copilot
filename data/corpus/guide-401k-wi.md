# 401(k) Guide — Wisconsin Participants

This guide supplements **faq-401k** with state-specific notes for employees who reside or are taxed in Wisconsin.

## Wisconsin State Tax Treatment

Wisconsin generally conforms to federal treatment of 401(k) contributions: pre-tax contributions reduce Wisconsin taxable wages. Roth 401(k) contributions are taxed in the year contributed; qualified Roth distributions are not taxed at distribution.

Wisconsin **does not currently offer** a state-level Roth IRA wrapper or saver's credit beyond the federal credit.

## Withdrawals and Penalties

Wisconsin does not impose additional state withholding on early withdrawals beyond the federal 10% penalty rules. State tax on distributions is handled at filing.

## Wisconsin Retirement System (WRS) Rollovers

Employees with prior service in the **Wisconsin Retirement System** (state, municipal, school, or UW) may roll over eligible balances into the Meridian Mutual 401(k) plan. The required form is **ET-2310** from the Wisconsin Department of Employee Trust Funds (etf.wi.gov), countersigned by Meridian Mutual's Total Rewards team. Allow 4–6 weeks for processing.

## Wisconsin-Specific Beneficiary Considerations

Wisconsin is a **community property state**. If you are married and you designate a beneficiary other than your spouse for any portion of your 401(k), your spouse must consent in writing on **Form MM-401-WI-SPB**. Notarization is required.

## Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDROs)

QDROs in Wisconsin are processed by the Fidelity QDRO Center. Provide the divorce decree and proposed QDRO to qdrocenter@fidelity.com; Meridian Mutual's plan administrator reviews within 10 business days.
