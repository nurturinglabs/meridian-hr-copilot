# Workplace Investigations Procedure

This procedure governs intake, investigation, and resolution of workplace complaints — including harassment, discrimination, retaliation, and other policy violations — at Meridian Mutual. It is restricted to HR Admin and Employee Relations roles.

## Intake

Complaints may originate through:

- **Ethics Hotline** (Navex EthicsPoint, 24/7, anonymous option)
- Direct to a manager (who must escalate to ER within 24 hours)
- Direct to Employee Relations
- Open Door to a People Partner

All intakes are logged in **Navex Case Management** with **Form ER-100** within 24 hours of receipt.

## Investigator Assignment

The Director, Employee Relations assigns an investigator within **3 business days** of intake. A conflict-of-interest screen is performed; investigators may not have a direct or skip-level reporting relationship to the complainant or respondent. Cases involving officers route to outside counsel.

## Interview Protocol

- **Order**: complainant first, witnesses next, respondent last
- **Setting**: separate, private; no joint interviews
- **Notice**: respondent receives written notice of the allegation no fewer than 24 hours before their interview, except where evidence preservation requires otherwise
- **Weingarten rights** apply to bargaining-unit employees; union representation must be permitted upon request
- **Recordings** are not permitted without express written consent

Contemporaneous notes are uploaded to Navex within 24 hours of each interview.

## Documentation and Legal Hold

Upon intake, **Legal initiates a litigation hold** on the email and chat accounts of named parties. Investigators must not destroy or alter any source documents. All artifacts are retained for **7 years** post-closure.

## Findings and Closure

Findings are categorized as:

- **Substantiated** — preponderance of the evidence supports the allegation
- **Unsubstantiated** — preponderance does not support the allegation
- **Inconclusive** — insufficient evidence

A **closure memo** is delivered to the Chief Human Resources Officer with corrective action recommendations to the manager and Legal. Both complainant and respondent receive an outcome notice (with appropriate confidentiality) within 5 business days.

## Retaliation Monitoring

ER monitors complainant and witness employment changes for **12 months** post-closure (PTO usage, performance ratings, transfers, terminations) and flags anomalies to the CHRO.

For internal questions, contact er-leads@meridianmutual.com.
